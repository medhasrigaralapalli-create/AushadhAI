The Lock & Key
Reimagined.

Every disease has a protein target. Every protein target has a pocket. AushadhAI uses PMDM dual diffusion to design molecules that fit those pockets with atomic-level precision — generating drug candidates in seconds, not years.

hub
What is a Protein Pocket?

A cavity on the protein surface where a drug molecule (ligand) can bind and modulate the protein's activity — like a keyhole waiting for the right key.

auto_awesome
What is QED Score?

Quantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (0–1). Above 0.6 = excellent candidate. Combines 8 molecular properties into one score used by pharma labs worldwide.

rule
Lipinski's Rule of 5

MW ≤500 · LogP ≤5 · H-bond donors ≤5 · H-bond acceptors ≤10. If a molecule passes all 4, it's likely orally bioavailable — a critical drug property.
